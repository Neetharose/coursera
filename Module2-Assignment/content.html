Missionary Sisters of Charity

History

The Missionary Sisters of Charity are an order begun in India through the wonderful response to a "call within a call" by Mother Theresa. God moved the heart of Theresa to seek out the poor by taking on the vows of poverty and chastity, to assimilate as far as possible with the same kind of poverty experienced by those in the slums and who experienced homelessness, poverty, sickness and little education. The "being among the poor" and living "with the poor" nurtures the work of missionary charity to better improve the lives of the poor. Prayer, fasting, and an active faith life and participation in the Sacraments of Eucharist and Penance are pivotal to their lives in serving the poor.

After School Sacrament Preparation

The Sisters are a major part of our parish and the Parish Religious Education and Sacrament Program.

All who are interested in having their children prepared for the sacraments are welcome to join the Sisters on Wednesday's and other designated days as part the children's enrolment and participation in Sacrament preparation. The program usually coincides with School Terms.

The sisters are also available in some situations in helping adults and older children to prepare for the sacraments. These preparation include for the sacraments of Baptism, Reconciliation, Eucharist and Confirmation. The sisters may include some explanation of the Sacrament of healing and Anointing of the Sick

If you are interested in the program please contact the Sisters on 9091 8938

Visitation

The Sisters have as a major part of their Religious Charism the visiting of the sick and poor. Home visitations from the sisters are most welcome and they may be contacted so as to invite them to your home for a visit or to let them know of someone you may feel could do with a visit from them.

Home Blessing

Home Blessings are most welcome and encouraged with the Sisters usually being the contact and organising of Home Blessings. As part of Home Blessings we greatly encourage as part of Home Blessings the Consecration to the Sacred Heart of the family home and family members.

Please contact the Sisters if you would like to have your home blessed.

Sacraments

Baptism

You may be seeking to enquire about having your child baptised into the Catholic Church. Preparation sessions occur on the second and fourth Tuesday of each month. The fourth Tuesday of December and the second Tuesday of January are the exception as all enjoy the Christmas festive season and New Year holiday time.

Each child's baptism will require the parents to attend a once only preparation session where important details for church registration are gathered, information about the meaning of baptism is shared, a short DVD called 'My Baby's Baptism' is shown and discussed, and a possible date established for the baptism.

Please contact the parish office if you require further information or would like to give notice of your attending one of the sessions.

Baptisms are held every Sunday at 11:30 to noon at St. Mary's Kalgoorlie and as arranged at other mass centres

Preparation Sessions occur at St. Mary's Hall on the 2nd and 4th Tuesday of each month at 4pm. Please confirm your intention to attend on 9021 2100

Healing and Anointing of the sick

Whether you or a friend are at home, in a hospital or visiting the parish and are seriously ill, whether bodily or mental illness, please do not hesitate to contact the parish and request to receive the Sacrament of Anointing.

Once called the "The Last Rites", the sacrament is not only for those who may be facing death strengthening them and giving them courage, but is a sacrament of healing for those who may be seriously ill and could die. In receiving the sacrament, through the grace of Christ, they may be healed of life threatening injuries and or sickness.

Marriage

The parish greatly encourages your enquiry, if you and or your intended are Catholic and you desire to marry in any of our Catholic Churches in the Goldfields parish region, then you are warmly invited to enquire. You may also be seeking to marry at another parish Catholic Church and by a priest from elsewhere. The respective parties to be married (man and woman) will be required to attend marriage preparation sessions with the priest over several weeks and or months. The sessions include the preparation of civil and church documents.

Early contact with your parish priest and or the priest who will be the celebrant at your marriage is essential, even before you have booked a place for the reception or the church.

Please do not hesitate to contact the parish office if you are a Catholic and are seeking to marry.

Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults and Children

The standard procedure for entering into and becoming a Catholic as an adult is through a period of time called the 'Catechumenate'. Saint Mary's Parish Kalgoorlie offers an RCIA/C program that begins in October and is completed at the Initiation ceremony at the Easter Vigil mass usually March/April. The fortnightly sessions are either weekly or fortnightly with a break during Christmas and the New Year.

If you are interested in this program, please contact the Parish Priest on 9021 2100

Acolytes

Our Acolyte ministry proudly serve at our Mass liturgies and in other duties such as communion to the sick. These specially chosen men meet all the requirements to serve in this ministry in the parish.

We warmly welcome enquiry from mature men who have a lengthy participation in parish life and think may like to serve in the Acolyte ministry

Altar Servers

Would you like to become an Altar Server?

Each year during the month of September the parish commits time to the training of young boys and girls toward the ministry of Altar Server. Each child though will need to have the permission and support of their parents via a signed form, will make a commitment to attend mass regularly, be prepared to be on an Altar Server roster, and may be asked to be part of some social events along with mini training seminars from time to time.

Training will occur on Wednesday's and Saturday's during September with a requirement to attend a commitment mass in early September and to receive installation on the feast of Christ the King usually occurring at the end of November.

Welcome to our youth page. We are known as Goldfields Youth Ministry (G.Y.M.). We are a group of young people aged between 13 to 18 years of age. We gather weekly to discuss aspects of the Christian faith, share ideas, and form bonds of friendship.

Our aim is to provide a community where young people feel welcome and be supported and formed in their Christian faith. As a youth group we strive to promote youth activities at St Mary's Catholic Parish which are fun and informative. We meet weekly on Monday evenings between 6:30 and 8:00 PM.

If you would like to be part of our group speak to one of the St Mary's priests or one of our youth representatives. You can also get in touch with us at our facebook page.

Nullarbor Mission

NULLARBOR MISSION TRIP 5.0

30 SEPTEMBER – 6 OCTOBER 2019

REFLECTIONS


Balladonia Station


Balladonia station had a padlock on its front gates upon arrival on the evening of Mon 30 Sept. The next morning (1 Oct) a further attempt to visit was successful through the providential arrival of a water truck at the same time as we arrived.

This year has been a particularly dry year on the Nullarbor and Balladonia station was drastically de-stocking at the time of this Mission Trip.

At the conclusion of the morning tea there was a spontaneous prayer for rain led by the visiting priest with a blessing of Greg, Cynthia and all those who work on the property.


Arubiddy Station


We arrived at Arubiddy Station Homestead in the late afternoon of Tues 1 Oct and were able to have a bit of a yarn with the new managers of the station.

As we hit the road the sun was just creeping below the horizon and it provided for a nice photo opportunity in front of the Station Homestead (see below for a photo of Fr Matthew with the Homestead in the background).


Eucla


We gathered together with the various Christian residents of Eucla upon arrival in Eucla on Wed 2 Oct gathered together. Among those present at this communal meal was the new community nurse together with her husband and 4 children. It was quite an experience being a part of a meal of 10 people. This never could have been imagined when these Mission Trips first re-started back in early 2017.

Although not necessarily Catholic, these Christians certainly appreciate having an official Church presence from time to time.

One of the employees of the Motel was again very happy to have someone to play golf with on this Mission Trip. Opportunities to get away from work surroundings and spend time playing sport etc. with a fresh face are few and far between and very much appreciated by the Eucla locals.


Forrest


Forrest is the most remote outpost in the Nullarbor Mission. It is a tiny community located on the Trans-Australian railway – 122 kms north-west of Eucla.

The 4WD track from Eucla to Forrest was tough going. Travelling from Eucla to Forrest, the 122 km journey took 4 hrs 15 mins. The return journey was quicker: 3 hrs 30 mins. This was the same route, it’s just that driver tolerance for rough terrain was higher on the return journey than on the outgoing journey!

The reason that a community exists at Forrest is because of Forrest Airport – a privately owned regional runway.

What keeps Forrest ticking over is aircraft re-fuelling. Smaller planes can’t make it all the way across Australia – they need somewhere to stop and re-fuel. Forrest is at a strategic location to meet this need. Both military and civilian aircraft make use of this re-fuelling capability at Forrest.

On average, 45 people per month make use of the accommodation facilities at Forrest. Of these, the vast majority arrive by air rather than land.

Railway workers also use Forrest as an accommodation base whilst they carry out maintenance work on the Trans-Australian railway.

The permanent population of Forrest is 2. These 2 people are the caretakers of the Forrest Airport/Bed & Breakfast operation.

Caretakers are employed on a 1-year contract.

Their role is a varied one – owing to the nature of the operation: airport management, hospitality, meteorological services. The current caretakers even reported experiences of having to rescue stray/trapped animals on the property!

The caretakers provided wonderful hospitality to us on our overnight stay on Fri 4 Oct. Forrest is a wonderful little haven on the far distant outskirts of the Goldfields Catholic Community.

See below for a photo of us at the Manager’s Residence.



Border Village


A number of familiar faces were present at Border Village on this Mission Trip – as well as some new ones as well. It was nice to catch up with all of them during our overnight stay at the Village on Sat 5 Oct. On the final morning of the Mission Trip a large spontaneous gathering of Catholics took place. There were 3 large Catholic families touring around Australia together. 2 people were able to join us for Sunday morning Mass in the Border Village Chapel. More would have attended the Mass had they not been in such a rush to hit the road for Perth as early as possible on that morning. Nevertheless, the whole cohort of about 15 people crammed into the chapel for a traveller’s blessing once Mass was ended. It was a special way to conclude this 2nd Mission Trip for 2019 and 5th Mission Trip overall (since re-inception).

The Archdiocese

The Archdiocese covers 471,118 sq. kms and has a population of about 1.5 million, of whom about 365,000 are Catholics. The bulk of the population is in the capital city, however, the Archdiocese also includes extensive fishing, agricultural, pastoral and mining industries.
The Archdiocese

John Paul College

John Paul College is a Catholic Co-educational College established in 1984 serving the Goldfields Community in Western Australia. From their foundations of PRENDIVILLE COLLEGE 'for Girls' established in 1971 and CHRISTIAN BROTHERS COLLEGE, CBC, 'for boys' Kalgoorlie established in 1906, they continue to educate young men and women within a Catholic framework of justice, peace and charity. These foundations form the College motto of Justice, Peace and Charity and underpin all activities and relationships they foster at the College.
John Paul College

St. Joseph's Boulder

St Joseph’s School Boulder is a Catholic primary school catering for over 240 children. The school offers an outstanding education for children from Three Year Old Pre Kindy to Year Six.
St Joseph’s School is founded on the traditions of the Sisters of St Joseph of the Sacred Heart. As a Catholic school, they uphold the values of Jesus Christ. The school has a proud tradition of valuing children from many ethnic backgrounds and reflects the multicultural diversity of Australia.
The school has a warm, intimate and friendly environment where all children are challenged to reach their potential in a Catholic context.
